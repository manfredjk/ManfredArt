Manfred Krautschneider BA, MSc, PhD, (Monash University), Dip Vis Art (Box Hill Institute)

Light is liquid - The Surreal Abstract Reflections.

 

My photographs observe reality in reflection, or tangentially, so as to acquire something surprising about us and our subject. To this end, I focus on streetscapes reflected on imperfect surfaces. The multiple reflections and transmission on and through windows, awnings, etc, produce smears of emotion and beautiful dramas. The subtle mnemonics in these ambiguous images, allow deep associations to emerge over time, and elicit unexpected connections with modern art. On the border between recognition and abstraction, these photographs also modify and fragment space, hinting at our own insubstantiality, and allowing a psycho-social reading, conjuring up primal fears of conflict, isolation and loss.

 

Rather than photos, I am more interested in these images as works of art, tending toward the psycho-social or a contemporary semi-abstract surrealism. In fact, one could easily mistake my photographs for contemporary paintings. Successfully holding the border between recognition and abstraction, my surreal photographs transform suburban streetscapes into transcendent images. The earlier of these works (2002-9) were shot on film with my Olympus OM2, and underwent a variety of in camera and in processing experiments to augment the image, including in-camera superimposition, and paper, exposure, and development interventions, including solarization, to remove / enhance detail and produce a “magical” print. Ironically, despite my later use of high-resolution Digital Cameras, and Photoshop, I have gone back to basics, shunning modifications except for cropping and occasional choice of contrast or saturation. The results are no less magical.
